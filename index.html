Summer School on Chromatic Homotopy Theory and Higher (Infinity-Categorical) Algebra

  • Shanghai Center for Mathematical Sciences & School of Mathematical Sciences, Fudan University
  • Yanqi Lake Beijing Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Applications
  • August 15–26, 2022

About

This year, the International Workshop on Algebraic Topology (IWoAT) remains a summer school, on the topics of chromatic homotopy theory and higher (infinity-categorical) algebra. This was originally planned for Summer 2020 and is designed to evolve for next installment. This year’s event is jointly sponsored by Shanghai Center for Mathematical Sciences & School of Mathematical Sciences, Fudan University and Yanqi Lake Beijing Institute of Mathematical Sciences and Applications. The two week event will take place fully online from August 15 to August 26, 2022 at zoom 388 528 9728 with passcode BIMSA.

Registration

To participate in the online summer school, please fill out a registration form here. If you have any questions, please write to Ms. Jiao Liu (刘娇，liujiao@fudan.edu.cn).

Schedule and Syllabus (updated August 8, 2022)

Zoom: 388 528 9728
Passcode: BIMSA
All talks will be China Standard Time (GMT+8)

Mon. Aug. 15, Day 1

8:20–8:30, Guozhen Wang, Opening remarks
8:30–9:30, Jacob Lurie, Opening talk
9:45–10:45, Sihao Ma, Chromatic Lecture 1: Cohomology theories, Brown representability, K-theory [notes]
11:00–12:00, Heyi Zhu, ∞-Categories Lecture 1: Simplicial sets, Kan complexes, categories, nerves, simplicial categories, infinity categories, homotopy categories, equivalences [notes]

Tue. Aug. 16, Day 2

8:30–9:30, David Gepner, Opening talk [notes]
9:45–10:45, Sihao Ma, Chromatic Lecture 2: Complex bordism, Thom construction [notes]

11:00–12:00, Heyi Zhu, ∞-Categories Lecture 2: Joins, slices, initial/final objects, limits/colimits, functor categories, uniqueness (up to contractible space of choices) [notes]

Wed. Aug. 17, Day 3

8:30–9:30, Ang Li, Chromatic Lecture 3: Spectra, smash product, ring spectra
10:00–11:00, Ang Li, ∞-Categories Lecture 3: Left/right fibrations, cartesian/cocartesian fibrations, set up the straightening functor

Thu. Aug. 18, Day 4

8:30–9:30, Yutao Liu,Chromatic Lecture 4: Complex orientations, formal groups, example of KU
9:45–10:45, Zhipeng Duan, Chromatic Lecture 5: MU as a universal example, calculations of MU*MU and E*MU
11:00–12:00, Yunze Lu, ∞-Categories Lecture 4: Adjoint functors, presentability, tensor product

Fri. Aug. 19, Day 5

8:30–9:30, Guchuan Li, Chromatic Lecture 6: Quillen's theorem on π*MU
10:00–11:00, Guchuan Li, Chromatic Lecture 7: Understand formal groups (over fields)

Mon. Aug. 22, Day 6

8:30–9:30, Zhipeng Duan, Chromatic Lecture 8: "Higher algebra" (ring spectra and modules), application: construct K(n), introduce E-theory
10:00–11:00, Yunze Lu, ∞-Categories Lecture 5: Smash products, stability, stabilization

Tue. Aug. 23, Day 7

8:30–9:30, Hana Jia Kong, ∞-Categories Lecture 6: Derived categories, Verdier sequences, t-structures
10:00–11:00, Weinan Lin, Chromatic Lecture 9: Thick subcategories, define type n, Balmer spectrum of finite spectra, state thick subcategory theorem

Wed. Aug. 24, Day 8

8:30–9:30, Weinan Lin, Chromatic Lecture 10: Nilpotence theorem, thick subcategory theorem
9:45–10:45, Foling Zou, ∞-Categories Lecture 7: Monoidal and symmetric monoidal structures
11:00–12:00, Foling Zou, ∞-Categories Lecture 8: Rings (associative and commutative) and modules

Thu. Aug. 25, Day 9

8:30–9:30, Yutao Liu, Chromatic Lecture 11: Construction of vn-self maps
10:00–11:00, Ningchuan Zhang, Chromatic Lecture 12: Bousfield localizations

Fri. Aug. 26, Day 10

8:30–9:30, Ningchuan Zhang, Chromatic Lecture 13: Monochromatic layers, chromatic convergence theorem
10:00–11:00, Hana Jia Kong, ∞-Categories Lecture 9: The Barr-Beck-Lurie theorem
11:00–11:10, Jie Wu, Closing remarks
